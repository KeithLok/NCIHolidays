About New York

The city lies at the mouth of the Hudson River in the southernmost part of the state, which is part of the Mid-Atlantic region of the U.S. The city spans a land area of 305 square miles. New York City has a population of approximately 8.2 million people. The New York Metropolitan Area, which spans lower New York, northern New Jersey, and southwestern Connecticut, has a population of 18.7 million, making it the largest metropolitan area in the U.S. As of 2007, it was 5th largest in the world, after Tokyo, Sao Paulo, Mexico City and Seoul. Being the most populous city in the world, New York is an enomormous city and trying to see all of city in one visit is almost impossible. Instead, go for the must-sees - the Empire State Building, the Statue of Liberty, Central Park, the Metropolitan Museum of Art.
New York City is a center for media, culture, food, fashion, art, research, finance, and trade. It has one of the largest and most famous skylines on earth, dominated by the iconic Empire State Building.
Flight Prices average €540 per person.
